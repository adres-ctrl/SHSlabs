CNS / PNS Boundary
Where the Central Nervous System Ends
The motor signal originates in the motor cortex (CNS) as an upper motor neuron (UMN) command. It travels down the corticospinal tract and crosses to the opposite side at the medullary pyramids (CNS). The signal synapses on a lower motor neuron (LMN) in the anterior horn of the spinal cord — still CNS. The moment the LMN axon exits through the ventral root, it becomes a peripheral nerve — PNS. For speech muscles controlled by cranial nerves, the boundary occurs where the nerve exits the brainstem.
Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) = CNS. Damage causes spastic paralysis — increased tone, hyperreflexia.
Lower Motor Neuron (LMN) = PNS. Damage causes flaccid paralysis — decreased tone, atrophy, fasciculations.
Contralateral control: the left motor cortex controls the right side of the body. Crossing occurs at the medullary pyramids.
