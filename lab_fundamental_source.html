Bernoulli's Principle at the Glottis
As airflow speeds up through the narrow glottis, pressure drops. This low pressure pulls the vocal folds inward — the aerodynamic half of MEAD. Dot spacing shows velocity: widely spaced dots = fast air = low pressure.

Frequency & Intensity
CT muscle tension controls vocal fold stiffness → changes F0 → perceived as pitch. Subglottal pressure (Psub) controls how far folds are pushed open → changes amplitude → perceived as loudness. Both affect the wave simultaneously.
